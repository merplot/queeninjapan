<H3>News of the Queen</h3> 
</center>
<br>

     <figure class="float-right img-xsmall">
  <img src="fc6_2.jpeg">
<figcaption>by Yuko Harunaga</figcaption>
</figure>

<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> Queen's long-awaited new album, <I>Dedicated to the World</i> [the Japanese title of <I>News of the World</i>], is finally complete, and I think it will already be on record stores by the time this issue comes out!
<br><br>
Here at the FC, we decided to gather representatives from Tokyo fan circles and have them listen to the album.  The coverart features a monster from the comic book <I>Hook</i> chasing fleeing humans, a completely different design from previous albums.  The band also all cut their hair, seemingly to change their image.
<br><br>
Now let's listen to each song one-by-one!
<br><br>
<br><I>We Will Rock You</i>
<br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "You're just a bunch of kids in the city doing what you want.  We'll teach you what real rock is.  One day you'll grow up and run the world."  It's hard to hear and I don't really understand it, but it feels like Queen's response to punk rock.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> The initial clapping has a certain leading feel to it, like the intro to John Lennon's <I>Give Peace a Chance.</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> The "don don chan!" rhythm emphasizes the third beat.  It's a fundamental rhythm of rock music, just like Brian said in an interview.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> I've already been hooked in from the beginning!
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It's not just the four of them clapping, the studio staff are also clapping.  I bet that was a lot of fun!
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> And then, just as Freddie finished leading the chorus, Brian takes center stage.  This part is really cool!  This is awesome.
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> This'll be perfect for concerts!  It's perfect for riling up the crowd.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> We should all sing along with this at concerts!
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Legendary Champions (We Are The Champions)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "We've been criticized by people and made mistakes.  We haven't committed any crimes, but we've been treated badly.  But we're champions!  We'll fight to the end.  Life isn't always rosy... let's fight to the very end.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It's a very Freddie-esque song.  It's the chip on his shoulder.  When the piano started playing and his voice came on, I got chills.  This is my favorite song on this LP... it's very moving.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> John's basslines at the beginning are very nice.  I think the classical style is very good.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> Brian's guitar is also great!  It seems like this song intentionally doesn't have a solo interlude, but it sounds very lush.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> The arrangement is great too.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Sheer Heart Attack</i>
<br><Br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> Sorry, I can't quite make out the lyrics.  But they seem to have a very punk feel to them.  I think the title is "Heart Attack".
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> This song is in a punk style.  It's a refreshing song, I like it.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> It's actually a more mature sound than punk...
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> The way the chorus comes in is really Queen-like.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> Roger's songs are usually the hardest of all Queen's songs, and they have an everyman feel to them, which is great.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> What is that signal-like song I hear along the way?
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> I think it's feedback.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>All Dead (All Dead, All Dead)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "It's all over, she's gone.  We had dreams back then, but now it's all over. Even the memories are gone."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> It's a well-done song.  The main melody is so strong that it feels like the arrangement was done later.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> The chorus is also good.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> The development of the melody is different from other pop songs.  People who don't like Queen will probably find the way the melodies connect to be unnatural.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> It's a great song with a melancholic note.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> There's no guitar at the beginning, so I was wondering what was going on, but then it came out in the middle and I was really happy to hear it.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Eternal Wings (Spread Your Wings)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "Every day is like watching the same show over and over again.  It's a boring life.  So throw away the ideas that are stuck in your head... spread your wings and fly away."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> It's a somewhat quaint song.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> It reminds me of a Japanese folk band that had a song that went, "I want to spread my wings and fly into the vast sky."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> John is a very good composer.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> I wish John would sing his songs.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> It's a pretty funky song.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> Still, the defining point of it is that it has a certain British sensibility.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> Freddie's voice has a different enunciation than others.  It's powerful and nice.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> And the guitar solo fades out at the end.  What a great solo!
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Hidden Flame (Fight From the Inside)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> I can't make this one out very well either.  The original title is "Fight With All Your Heart".
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> I like Roger's two tracks the best on this LP so far.  They're very hard and we haven't heard anything like them in a while.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> I want to make special mention of John's bass.  I never thought he'd play such a driving bassline.  He's such a great bassist.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> The guitar is half-muted to create an effect.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Get Down, Make Love</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> I'm too embarrassed to translate this one.  Please enjoy translating it yourself.  It's a song that lives up to its title!  The fantastic part that appears in the second half is a perfect expression of that very essence through sound.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> Was that sound was made with synthesizers?
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> No, it sounds like it was made by speeding up the tape or something like that.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> I've always wondered why Roger's drums are so heavy, but I think it really works in this song.  It seems like the trend in rock music right now is for drums to be very crisp and light.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It feels like an avante-garde piece.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> I think this is one of the ones where they try to show that they're attempting new things.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>A Hollow Life (Sleeping on the Sidewalk)</i>
<br><br><Br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "When I was little, all I had to play with was a trumpet.  When I wandered around the city and saw how wealthy adults lived, I never wanted to go home again."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It reminds me of Bad Company's "Can't Get Enough".
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> I think it sounds more like Climax Blues Band.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It's in the same vein as <I>See What A Fool I've Been</i>, which hasn't been released in Japan yet.  So it's not surprising.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> Modern blues, so-to-speak.  Brian is dedicated to playing blues chords properly.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span>  I think this guitar is a Les Paul.  I think <I><Tie Your Mother Down</i> was also a Les Paul.
<br><br><br><br>
<i>Where Love Goes (Who Needs You)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "I came at 6:30 as promised, but you didn't come til 7:00.  I trusted you, but you betrayed me.  You walked all over me.  My pride has been hurt.  I can't take it anymore.  I've made up my mind, I don't need you anymore."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> This would be fun to do onstage, too.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> Is John playing this guitar?
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> I don't hear any bass.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> No, I think this is Brian.  The Spanish guitar is really good.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It's a really fun song with a South American flair, but the arrangement is so Queen-esque that I couldn't help but laugh.  It's a really good song.  John might be the one to bring change to Queen in the future!
<br><br><br><br>
<I>It's Late</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "You said you loved me, but I didn't, and I have myself to blame.  If you still love me... no, it's too late, too late.  Do we really have to break up?  It's too late."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> It starts off romantically but gradually builds to a big chorus, a typical Queen song.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> The chorus is also multi-tracked.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> This is the song that has the most Queen flamboyance, and yet it's also melodic.
<br><br><br><br>
<I>Melancholy Blues (My Melancholy Blues)</i>
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> "I feel like something has ended and I've found a new life.  I don't really want to talk about it now.  I feel like I've sung a bit of melancholy blues."
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> Wow, what is this song?  Is it really Queen?
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> It sounds a bit like old-timey movie music.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> It's a piano trio without a guitar.  Roger uses brushes instead of sticks, so it's an unusual song.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> This is Freddie's time to shine.  He could've sung a big chorus, but instead he sings softly.  Freddie truly is a great vocalist.
<br><br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> So, what was your overall impression?
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">B:</span> It has a variety of songs and feels a little disjointed, but it's a really fun album.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">C:</span> They've been doing symphonic rock for a long time but it felt here like they were just doing what they wanted to in a really relaxed manner.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">D:</span> I thought Roger's performances were stellar.  He's definitely a hard rocker.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">E:</span> Brian also seems to be enjoying playing freely.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">F:</span> John wrote two songs on this one, so I'm looking forward to what's to come.
<br><br><br>
<span style="font-weight: 900">A:</span> Thank you very much!
