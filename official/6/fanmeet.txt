The young people who gathered at Tokyo's Yomiuri Hall on March 22nd were wearing spring attire, and the whole venue seemed to light up.  The Queen Fan Club Spring Gathering kicked off with a bang!  As soon as the doors opened, people poured in and headed straight for the merchandise sales corner, where they bought aprons, stationery sets, calendars, posters, and iron-on patches.  Everything sold out within 20 minutes.  If you didn't make it in time, please use the order sheet in the back of this issue.
<br><br>
As always, Warner's Hamasaki hosted one film screening after the other, and each time there were thunderous shouts of "Roger" "Freddie!" "Brian!" "John!"  It was a real struggle after the screening.  When the staff returned exhausted to their Matsui Building office, the hall was

The next meetup was at the Osaka National Auditorium, so we hopped on the shinkansen [bullet train], passing by all the sakura blossoms.  This hall is an incredible building, built before the war, and apparently served as a place where you could once spend "A Night at the Opera".  So even though one could say that it was made for a Queen event, the roar of the crowd inside was so intense it felt like the ceiling might collapse at any second!  The host was Hayashi from Warner.  "I promised my superiors I would make <I>Somebody to Love</i> a hit, and it was.  And I've promised to make <I>te wo toriatte</i> another hit.  I hope you all can help us out with this!" he said enthusiastically.
<br><br>
Next, we headed south to Fukuoka.  Brrr, it was cold down there!  The host at Daihakata Hall on the 28th was the lovely Miss Koyama, who looked great in her long dress.  The audience was quiet, perhaps to match Miss Koyama's quiet demeanor, but the excitement picked up in the second half, and they even showed two encore screenings of Queen's film!  It really illustrated the energy of the people of Hakata.
