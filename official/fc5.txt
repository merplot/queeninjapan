<h2>Vol. 5 &mdash; Autumn 1976 </h2>


     <figure class="float-right img-small">
  <img src="5/fc5_3.png">

</figure>


Vol. 5 is billed as a "Queen's Concert Special Feature" and contains pictures and info from their four UK tour dates in September 1976.  There is also a note from Roger translated into Japanese, as well as a Queen History page, full of important dates in the band's history.
<br><br>
A Freddie-produced single for Eddie Howell, titled <I>Man from Manhattan</i>, is mentioned in a short article.  Brian also played guitar on the single.  It's also explained that the <a href="../promo.html#lonely">Japanese single release</a> of <I>See What A Fool I've Been</i> that was mentioned in the prior issue has been cancelled "due to unforeseen circumstances", with no elaboration.  The same article mentions that the tapes for <I>A Day At The Races</i> have not been received in Japan yet, so the Japanese album release will be pushed back to January 1977.
<br><br>
The age-old brawl between fans who like a band for their music vs. fans who like a band for their looks and style seems to be a hot topic in this issue.  An opinion piece written by the Fan Club staff themselves comes to the conclusion that "unfortunately, we have to say that Queen fans are superficial."  A bitter letter that appears in the members' section notes a fan's decision to leave the fan club due to lack of substance.  Another, more cheerful letter writes about a missed connection&mdash;apparently they supposedly had an encounter with a blond foreign man who looked just like Roger and were wondering if anyone else had run into this stranger. 
   <br><br>
<a href="5/lookingback.html">Looking Back on 1976</a> &mdash; by Hamasaki of Warner Pioneer
